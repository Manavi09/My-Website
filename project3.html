Fleet Risk & Fuel Efficiency Analytics

This project demonstrates how to build a big data pipeline and interactive Tableau dashboards to analyze fleetwide driver performance, truck efficiency, and associated risks. Leveraging Cloudera’s Hadoop ecosystem, we ingested CSV data into HDFS, created tables in Hive, calculated key metrics (scaled risk and MPG), and visualized the results in Tableau.

Key Steps & Technologies

Data Ingestion & Storage
Converted multiple Excel sheets to CSV files and transferred them to a Cloudera Virtual Machine using scp.
Moved CSV files into HDFS with hdfs dfs -put, enabling distributed storage.
Created empty tables in Hive and loaded data from HDFS into these tables for efficient querying.

Data Transformation
Generated two critical metrics:
Scaled Risk: (events / total_miles) normalized to a 1–10 scale.
Overall MPG: (total_miles / total_gas).
Joined multiple tables in Hive to produce a comprehensive dataset covering driver details, truck attributes, and operational events (idling, risky incidents, etc.).

Visualization & Insights
Connected Hive to Tableau, where dashboards highlight:

• Risky Drivers: Ranking by scaled risk.
• Low MPG Trucks: Identifies specific truck IDs with below‐average fuel efficiency.
• Risky Events & Idling: Geographic maps showing where incidents and prolonged idling occur.
• Risky Models: Bubble chart illustrating which truck manufacturers or models have higher aggregated risk.

Enables stakeholders to pinpoint drivers with elevated risk, trucks with poor fuel economy, and regions with frequent incidents—supporting data‐driven decisions around maintenance, driver training, and route planning.

Outcome

By combining Hadoop’s scalability with Tableau’s visual analytics, the project provides a cohesive view of operational risks and inefficiencies across a large trucking fleet. This solution helps fleet managers proactively address high‐risk behaviors, optimize fuel usage, and improve overall safety and performance.
