Life Expectancy Analysis with Python

Overview

This two-phase project uses Python-based data science techniques to explore and predict life expectancy across various countries. By combining data preprocessing, exploratory analysis, and machine learning models, each phase offers deeper insights into the key factors impacting global health and longevity.

Phase 1: Data Exploration & Preprocessing

Dataset Cleaning
Imported a public life expectancy dataset containing indicators like GDP, immunization rates, healthcare spending, and lifestyle factors.
Addressed missing values and outliers, ensuring a cleaner, more consistent foundation for analysis.

Exploratory Analysis
Employed Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, and Seaborn to visualize relationships between various socio-economic and health features—such as income level or immunization coverage—and life expectancy.
Generated descriptive statistics and correlation heatmaps to pinpoint significant predictors (e.g., GDP, adult mortality, alcohol consumption).

Insights
Confirmed that well-funded healthcare systems and higher vaccination rates correlate strongly with increased life expectancy.
Identified certain demographic or lifestyle variables (e.g., smoking rates) that negatively impact national averages.

Phase 2: Machine Learning & Prediction

Model Building
Set up regression models (e.g., Linear Regression, Random Forest) to predict life expectancy based on the cleaned dataset.
Evaluated models using R², Mean Squared Error (MSE), and cross-validation to measure both accuracy and generalizability.

Feature Engineering & Tuning
Experimented with additional transforms, such as log-scaling high-range variables (GDP, population), to boost predictive performance.
Performed hyperparameter tuning on ensemble methods (Random Forest, Gradient Boosting) to refine predictions.

Results
Demonstrated that ensemble models often outperform basic linear approaches, yielding more robust predictions across countries.
Identified the top contributing factors—like adult mortality, GDP, and health expenditure—that drive variations in life expectancy outcomes.

Tools & Technologies

Python: Data processing and modeling
Pandas & NumPy: Cleaning and organizing large datasets
Matplotlib & Seaborn: Visual exploration of key features
Scikit‐learn: Regression models, cross‐validation, and hyperparameter tuning
Jupyter Notebook: Iterative data science workflow and documentation

Project Highlights

Rigorous data cleaning and outlier detection methods to ensure reliability.
Comprehensive visual insights, from correlation heatmaps to box plots, illustrating the drivers of life expectancy.
Multiple regression approaches—both traditional and ensemble‐based—to understand and forecast global health trends.

Conclusion

By breaking the workflow into two complementary phases—thorough exploration in Phase 1 and predictive modeling in Phase 2—this project delivers a holistic view of what shapes life expectancy worldwide. Policymakers, healthcare professionals, and data scientists alike can leverage these findings to inform strategies improving global population health.
