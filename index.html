Projects

Life Expectancy Analysis Using Python

In this project, I explored global life expectancy data to uncover what socioeconomic, demographic, and healthcare factors most strongly impact how long people live around the world. Employing Python’s data analytics ecosystem (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn), I cleaned and transformed raw data, filled in missing values, and performed descriptive and inferential analysis.

Key Highlights

  • Data Cleaning & Exploration: Handled missing data systematically, identified outliers, and used visualization techniques to spot trends.
  • Correlation & Regression: Investigated correlations between life expectancy and variables like GDP, healthcare spending, and literacy rates. Built regression models (e.g., Linear Regression) to predict life expectancy based on these factors.
  • Data Visualization: Created clear, compelling plots and charts that highlighted global discrepancies in life expectancy and offered insights into policy changes that might improve overall lifespans.
  • Outcome: Demonstrated how data-driven decision-making can inform public health initiatives, underscore inequalities, and guide resource allocation to improve longevity across different regions.

Tools & Technologies: Python, Jupyter Notebook, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Seaborn
